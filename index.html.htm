About Phöbus

In a world where data has become the most valuable currency, privacy is no longer optional. It is essential. We are a collective dedicated to advancing Zero-Knowledge development and implementation, furthering technologies that allow private individuals to prove their identities without the need to expose confidential information. As the internet evolves, so must our society toward a future where personal data remains truly personal, where trust is established through mathematics rather than intermediaries, and where access to privacy is not a privilege, but a universal right. We believe in a more democratic digital landscape, one where users regain control, systems are transparent by design, and innovation serves freedom rather than surveillance.

What is Zero-Knowledge?

What is Zero-Knowledge?

Zero-Knowledge (ZK) technology is a way to prove that something is true without revealing the underlying information. Imagine being able to prove you are over 18 without ever exposing your age. That's the core idea behind ZK: sharing proof, not data. By using advanced cryptography, ZK systems allow people and applications to interact securely while keeping sensitive information private. This approach is shaping a new generation of digital tools where trust doesn't require exposing yourself. This is the next great step for post-modern democracy.

Orion's Belt (temp)

"The app is a privacy-first, ephemeral messaging system where users communicate without accounts, phone numbers, or persistent identities by generating temporary cryptographic IDs and creating on-demand “rooms” for each conversation; messages are end-to-end encrypted on the client side, while the server acts only as a blind relay that cannot read or store any meaningful data, and once a room expires, all messages, identifiers, and keys are permanently deleted—ensuring that conversations leave no recoverable history and that even the intermediary has zero access to the content or metadata beyond transient routing."
